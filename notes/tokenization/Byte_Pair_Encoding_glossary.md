# Byte Pair Encoding Glossary

| Term | Definition | Context / Example |
|------|------------|-------------------|
| **Subword Unit** | A piece of a word that is smaller than the whole word but larger than a single character. | "lowest" -> "low" and "est" |
| **Morpheme** | The smallest meaningful morphological unit of a language that cannot be further divided. | "un" + "break" + "able" |
| **Phoneme** | Any of the perceptually distinct units of sound in a specified language that distinguish one word from another. | /p/, /b/, /d/, and /t/ in English (pad, pat, bad, bat). |
| **Truecasing** | The process of restoring the correct capitalization to text (e.g., at the start of sentences). | "barack obama" -> "Barack Obama" |
| **Cognate** | Words that have a common etymological origin and often sound/look similar across languages. | "claustrophobia" (EN) and "Klaustrophobie" (DE) |
| **Transliteration** | The process of transferring a word from the alphabet of one language to another. | "Барак Обама" (Russian) -> "Barack Obama" (English) |
| **Unigram F1 Score** | The harmonic mean of precision and recall calculated on single words (unigrams), ignoring sentence structure. | Used in the paper to evaluate exactly how many rare words were translated correctly. |
